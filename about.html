About Me

My name is Ricardo M. Martinez and I am a 23-year old aspiring software engineer from Miami, FL. In May 2017, I graduated from Columbia University's Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science with a B.S. in Computer Science. By taking classes covering a broad range of subject material, I discovered that I was most drawn to the "agile software development" work flow, where I was working collaboratively with others and developing a product with changing requirements.

One such project that I worked on is ImHere, which is an online attendance manager designed for use in a classroom setting. By combining different technologies and developing for a specific customer's use cases, my team and I were able to win JPMorgan Chase & Co.'s 2016 Self-Defined Project Award. You can learn more about the project in the 'Skills & Projects' section above.

One of my hobbies is exploring all things "Linux". When I'm not configuring various desktop environments or tiling window managers, I'm always looking for new niche software that could improve my computing experience. Recently, I've discovered rtv, ncdu, and pass.

Outside of the tech world, I like watching sports (particularly basketball and soccer), exercising by running around South Florida, and growing my still-very-small garden. I also like staying in touch with my grandparents and uncle who live in Germany, forcing me to keep my German in tip-top shape!

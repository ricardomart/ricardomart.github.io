About Me

My name is Ricardo M. Martinez and I am a 24-year old software engineer from Miami, FL. In May 2017, I graduated from Columbia University's Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science with a B.S. in Computer Science. The following year, I started working as a software developer at Tradebyte Software GmbH, in a small town in Germany called Ansbach. The change was dramatic - I was experiencing a new country and a new culture, all while working at my first software development job - but I quickly got into the swing of things, and before long, I was efficiently working with my team on all stages of our applications' development lifecycles.

Since I started working at Tradebyte, I've had the fortune of working on a project that introduced me to a number of topics that I wasn't previously familiar with. Technology-wise: containerization, continuous integration and deployment, advanced knowledge of a web framework (Django), and cloud computing (AWS); organizationally: agile methodologies, kanban boards, JIRA, and Confluence, coordinating the inter-process communication of two large software systems, and even working in teams of more than 3 people. I've also been tasked with presenting that same project to other developers, other departments within the company, and then even to clients of the company, all of which have helped me to explain technical implementations both at very detailed and general levels.

My hobbies in tech include exploring all things "Linux". When I'm not configuring various desktop environments or tiling window managers, I'm always looking for new niche software that could improve my computing experience. Recently, I've discovered rtv, ncdu, and pass.

Outside of the tech world, I like watching sports (particularly football, basketball, and soccer), exercising by running around my city or (more recently) bouldering, and growing my still-very-small garden. I also like staying in touch with my grandparents and uncle who all live in Hamburg, visiting them whenever I can find the time!
